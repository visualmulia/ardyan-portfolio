Questions & Answers

Frequently Asked Questions

Do we need to have meetings?

No. All communication is async via email and Notion. I work best with written briefs and documented feedback. You review on your own time, I build on mine. If you need real-time hand-holding, I'm not the right fit — and that's okay.

If you use AI, why isn't it cheaper?

AI helps me type code 3x faster — but that's not what you pay for. You pay for: 1. System architecture that won't break at scale, 2. Database design that handles edge cases, 3. Logic validation that prevents costly bugs, and 4. 15 years of design thinking applied to your product. AI is my power tool. You're hiring the architect, not the hammer.

How do I track progress without meetings?

Daily written updates in Notion. Live preview links. Sentry monitoring for real-time error tracking. You'll have more visibility than most traditional agencies — just without the Zoom fatigue.

What if I need changes after delivery?

30-day async support is included. Send feedback via email or Notion comments. I iterate within 24-48 hours. For major changes beyond scope, we discuss it as a new project phase.

How do you calculate project pricing?

Based on scope complexity and architectural requirements — not hours. A landing page might take 2 days but require minimal architecture. A SaaS with booking logic takes 2 weeks because conflict-free reservation systems need careful design.

What is the typical project development timeline?

2-4 weeks for most projects. Speed comes from AI acceleration + clear async communication. Delays usually happen when requirements change mid-build — which is why the discovery phase is critical.

Do you offer revisions and ongoing maintenance?

30-day support is included. Ongoing maintenance is available as a retainer (Rp 1.800.000/month or $100/month) — which includes monitoring, bug fixes, and minor updates.

What are your primary communication channels?

Email and Notion. All decisions are documented, and all feedback is threaded. No "wait, what did we agree on?" — it's all in writing.

How do you guarantee uptime for automations?

Sentry monitoring + automated health checks + fallback architectures. If a scraper fails, I know before you do. 99.9% is for automation tools — web apps depend on hosting (I recommend Vercel/Railway for 99.9%+).
